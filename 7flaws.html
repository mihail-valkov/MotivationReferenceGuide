Daniel H. Pink – DRIVE
Seven Deadly Flaws of If–Then Rewards
Why carrots & sticks backfire in knowledge work and creative environments.
1

They can extinguish intrinsic motivation

Paying for what people already enjoy can erode the joy of doing it for its own sake.

2

They can diminish performance

Rewards narrow focus—helping simple tasks but hurting creativity, problem‑solving, and complex work.

3

They can crush creativity

Fear of losing the reward—or chasing it—kills experimentation, risk‑taking, and originality.

4

They can crowd out good behaviour

People do the minimum for the reward and ignore unmeasured but vital work (helping teammates, long‑term thinking).

5

They can encourage cheating and shortcuts

Focus on the prize invites gaming the system—cooking numbers, cutting corners, unethical choices.

6

They can become addictive

Once introduced, rewards are expected every time—raising costs and weakening sustainable motivation.

7

They can foster short‑term thinking

Carrots and sticks push immediate compliance at the expense of lasting engagement, learning, and growth.

Healthier alternative: AMP

Use Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose to fuel durable motivation for engineers and creators.

Autonomy

Choice, self‑direction, and ownership of how work gets done.

Mastery

Deliberate practice, clear feedback, and stretch challenges.

Purpose

Connect work to meaningful impact beyond the individual.
